# Swedish legal basis

## Applicable legislation

### Offentlighets- och sekretesslagen (2009:400), chapter 24

All register microdata processed in this research is protected under the
Swedish Public Access to Information and Secrecy Act. Chapter 24 governs
secrecy in statistical activities and restricts disclosure of individual-level
data.

### Lag (2001:99) om den officiella statistiken

The Official Statistics Act regulates the production and dissemination of
official statistics in Sweden. SCB operates under this act and provides
secure-environment access for research purposes.

## Secure-environment access

### MONA (Microdata Online Access)

SCB's MONA platform provides remote access to microdata in a secure
computational environment. All computation occurs on SCB servers; no microdata
leaves the secure perimeter.

### SAFE (Secure Access For Enterprises)

The SAFE platform extends secure access to institutional researchers with
enhanced computational capabilities.

### Access policy

- Researchers must hold an approved research project.
- Data access agreements are signed between the research institution and SCB.
- All outputs are subject to statistical disclosure control before release.
- No individual-level data, no derived person-level outputs, and no model
  artefacts trained on real microdata are exported from the secure environment.

## Etikprovningsmyndigheten

This repository does not include any personal data and therefore does not by
itself require ethical approval under Etikprovningslagen (2003:460). Downstream
empirical analyses on linked microdata operated by the Department of Economics
are covered by a separate ethical approval obtained from
Etikprovningsmyndigheten where applicable.

## Riksarkivet preservation

Release artefacts (PDF/A-2u reports, CSV with UTF-8 BOM, Parquet) are produced
in formats compatible with Riksarkivet RA-FS 2009:1 preservation requirements.

## European Statistics Code of Practice 2.0

The research adheres to the ESS Code of Practice principles, including
professional independence, statistical confidentiality, and methodological
soundness. SCB's implementation of the Code of Practice governs all
microdata access.
